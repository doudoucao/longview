# Investment Research Standards

## Boundary

Longview research should improve research process quality, transparency, and collaboration. Unless the user explicitly scopes a regulated advisory workflow with proper review, do not produce personalized investment advice.

Avoid:

- unsupported buy/sell/hold conclusions
- price targets without methodology and assumptions
- certainty language around future returns
- claims that imply suitability for a specific investor

## Claim Types

Label claims as one of:

- Fact: directly supported by a reliable source.
- Estimate: derived from data or model assumptions.
- Opinion: attributed judgment from a source or analyst.
- Inference: reasoned conclusion drawn from evidence.
- Recommendation: proposed action for Longview's product, research process, or operating model.

## Financial Evidence

For company or security research, prefer:

- SEC/EDGAR or equivalent regulatory filings
- company investor relations materials
- audited financial statements
- exchange and regulator materials
- clearly sourced market data

For framework or product research, prefer official docs, repositories, release notes, changelogs, and direct product materials over summaries.

## Auditability

Research artifacts should make it possible for another analyst to reconstruct:

- which sources were used
- which claims came from which sources
- which assumptions were made
- which claims remain uncertain
- what a human reviewer should check before publication

## Safety Language

Use process-oriented recommendations such as "Longview should test this workflow" or "This is a candidate architecture." Avoid language that tells a reader what to trade.
